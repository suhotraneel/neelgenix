The Right to Look

Audio-Visual Experience

Right to Look is an immersive audio-visual art gallery experience. By scanning a QR code, visitors access a web app that guides them through the exhibition. Curated audio tours accompany each artwork, allowing users to explore the museum, discover the stories behind each piece, and hear directly the curators' voices.

Based on Double Diamond & 3 Phases of IB MYP Design Cycle.

2. Analyze

Scrutinize data, find patterns, define the problem. Synthesize to create a clear problem statement.

3. Develop

Research & Findings

Domain Study

Exploration of the contemporary art gallery landscape revealed key insights:

  • Visitor Engagement: Need for more than just visual observation.
  • Description vs. Guidance: Analysis of minimal descriptions vs guided experiences.
  • Immersive Experiences: Growing trend in artistic & cultural settings.
  • Audio-Visual Installations: Success of multi-sensory exhibits.

User Insights (Secondary Findings)

  • Common visitor disengagement due to lack of artwork context.
  • Existing audio guides are underutilized or lack personalization.
  • Desire for a cohesive narrative throughout an exhibition.
  • Lack of interaction with digital space.

Target Group

Individuals, aged 18-45, visiting or present in Mumbai, including students, employed professionals, and entrepreneurs with a casual interest in art exhibitions.

Problem Statement

"Art gallery visitors often lack sufficient contextual information and engaging experiences to fully appreciate artworks and understand the artists' intentions. This results in a diminished connection with the art, and therefore, reduced lasting engagement and visitor retention for the gallery."

Solution Concept

An immersive and accessible web application, with a seamless QR code onboarding process, that delivers curated audio-visual experiences for art exhibitions, providing deeper contextual information about artworks and artists to enhance visitor engagement.

Key Features

Inclusive Design

Integrating audio descriptions and visuals for broader accessibility.

Intuitive Interface

Inspired by familiar platforms like Pinterest and Spotify for ease of use.
